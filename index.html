Interests

I spend most of my time teaching architectural courses at my university. My main interests in architecture are environmental psychology and housing. I love to teach my students the reciprocal nature of our person-environment relationship so their design can improve the interaction between human and their built environment

Urban housings in Indonesia has been the main topic in my reseraches since I was in graduate school for master degree. I employ several frameworks, including positivist and non-positivist research design, to study the underlying pattern and complex-adaptive systems of urban housing in Indonesia. I approach my study using space syntax analytical method, diagram analysis, and social-ecological analysis.

Recently, the digital space becomes my new side interest in architectural teaching. In 2021, I conducted research to study how virtual reality can be used as a method for architectural building exploration. I explore the usage of free and open source software (FOSS) in the workflow of virtual reality production for architectural purposes.

Sometimes I also investigate computational design approach in architecture. My favorite tool is Blender. It is free and open source yet versatile to solve various design problems. The render engine and modifier tools are the features I love the most. I also like to tinker with its parametric modelling plugin, Sverchok, for fun purposes :)
